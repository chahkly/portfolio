Discovering the Need for Reporting and Change Logs in an Enterprise Product

Qualitative and quantitative research to validate feature requirements for transparency and traceability.

Research Question

Do enterprise users need reporting and change-log functionality in the product, and what requirements would such features need to meet to be valuable?

Context

The company develops algorithmic software for intelligent planning in a B2B enterprise environment. To enable direct user research, awareness for UX research had to be created among customers and internal stakeholders.

User Research Promotion

User research was introduced during a customer event where the research practice and upcoming studies were presented to enterprise customers. To support recruitment, a dedicated webpage explaining the purpose of user research was created in collaboration with marketing.

Additionally, marketing promoted the research initiative and the specific study through a customer newsletter. These activities helped establish initial contact with interested customers.

Recruitment

Through this outreach and collaboration with consultants and marketing, five enterprise customers were recruited for the study.

Methods

  • Kickoff sessions with participating companies
  • Qualitative user interviews
  • Complementary survey to validate insights

Findings

The study revealed a strong need for transparency and traceability in planning decisions.

Users required:

  • Reporting capabilities
  • Visibility of planning changes
  • Traceability of decisions for internal stakeholders and management

Impact

The research provided evidence for introducing reporting and change-tracking functionality.

The insights became the basis for the product team's decision to implement a reporting tool.

The feature is currently in development and is planned to be validated through usability testing with users.
